Let’s look at an example of customized learning for one of our clients. Click Case study to read about a solution built for a Travel and Transportation client.

Baggage Sortation Awareness
Background

American Airlines approached IBM to develop a learning solution that will help refresh their baggage handling team's knowledge of baggage sortation rules and regulations, by applying them in scenarios that they would encounter while at work.

Requirement

A skill refresher for baggage handlers in an International airport on managing different classifications of baggage.

Proposed Solution

An HTML5/Javascript based game that simulates the full cycle of baggage handling at airports (from checking in to unloading at conveyor), and gives the learners the opportunity to earn skill badges. The learners earn points for successfully completing activities. These points are aggregated into badges. In addition, they are given the opportunity to earn additional bonus points.

Rationale

The skill being refreshed is highly application based and covers a large number of variables. To keep it interesting and relevant for an adult learner, a game-based approach would be optimal.

Challenges

AA uses a highly restrictive learning framework called SORA and it takes a large amount of ground-up coding to build a fully functional game into it that also communicates with the LMS.
